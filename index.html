Aromo – Crafted Bites, Global Taste

QSR Case Study Report

Executive Summary

Aromo is a modern quick-service momo venture blending authentic flavors with creative cultural twists. With fusion ethnic presentations like kulhads, live counters, eco-friendly packaging, and pocket-friendly platters, Aromo brings together quality, affordability, and innovation. Starting from Guwahati, the aim is to expand globally, focusing on healthy, sustainable, and culturally rich dining experiences.

Business Model

The Aromo business model is a Quick Service Restaurant (QSR) format focusing on low-cost, high-quality food with maximum profitability. Fusion flavors, children-friendly menus, and travel-friendly packaging are central to the model.

[Business Model Diagram Placeholder]

Vision and Mission

Vision: To become the most loved global momo brand with a cultural fusion identity.

Mission: To provide authentic, affordable, and innovative momo experiences blending health, culture, and sustainability.

Product Portfolio

Aromo offers:

  • Veg momos (atta, ragi, millet-based)
  • Special Jain momos with separate utensils
  • Dessert momos (coconut jaggery, chocolate)
  • Pocket-friendly platters with beverages
  • Travel-friendly branded momo boxes with eco-packaging

Financial Projections

Projected ROI within 18–24 months, with 20–25% profit margins in year 1 scaling up to 35% by year 3.

Cost of Operations

Includes raw materials, labor, rent, utilities, branding, licensing, taxes, and IT infrastructure. Sustainability practices will reduce long-term costs.

Revenue Projections & Calculations

Estimated average ticket size: ₹120–₹150. With 200 customers/day in Guwahati, monthly revenue projected at ₹7–9 lakhs. Expansion to metro cities will increase customer base by 3–5x.

Revenue Model

Revenues from dine-in, takeaways, online deliveries, catering, and franchise fees. Upselling beverages and dessert momos ensures additional profits.

Franchise Model

Franchise partners will handle daily operations while Aromo central management will control brand standards, supply chain, and IT systems.

Organizational Structure

Corporate management ensures consistent brand quality across outlets, with clear franchisee responsibilities.

[Organizational Structure Diagram Placeholder]

Customer Preferences and USP

USP: fusion ethnic presentation, Jain-special menu, eco-friendly packaging, travel-friendly boxes, and child-friendly menu options. Focus on low cost + high quality.

Market Segmentation

Target audience: Students, young professionals, families, and health-conscious individuals. Expansion strategy focuses first on Guwahati, then metros, and later global markets.

Product Positioning

Aromo positions itself as a cultural fusion QSR delivering premium experiences at affordable prices.

Winning Strategies

Innovative menu, strong branding, localized tastes, eco-friendly initiatives, and loyalty programs.

Marketing Strategies

Social media campaigns, student discounts, influencer tie-ups, festival-based promotions, and offers starting at ₹39.

IT Strategies

POS integration, mobile ordering, data-driven customer insights, and cloud-based supply chain management.

Menu

  • Classic Veg Momos
  • Ragi & Atta Momos
  • Jain Momos (special)
  • Dessert Momos (coconut jaggery, chocolate)
  • Kids’ Special Momos
  • Beverages from ₹39 (iced tea, lemon soda, shakes)

Cost Breakdown

Food cost 30%, labor 20%, rent 15%, branding 10%, IT & licenses 5%, profit margin 20%.

Shop and Business Layout

[Business Layout Diagram Placeholder]

Modern ethnic interiors with live momo counters, kulhad serving, and eco-friendly furniture.

Logo and Branding

Aromo logo will reflect cultural fusion with modern typography. Packaging and momo boxes will be eco-friendly, travel-ready, and include branded dips.

Conclusion

Aromo is not just a momo brand—it’s a cultural experience designed to scale from Guwahati to the world. With strong financials, innovative menu, sustainability, and smart IT systems, Aromo is set to become a global QSR success story.
